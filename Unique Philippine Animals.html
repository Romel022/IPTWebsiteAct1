Animal Kingdom

Unique Animals Only Found in the Philippines

 
 Across different countries in the world, animal wildlife in the Philippines is considered as one of the most diverse. Across the 7,000 plus island in the
country,unique animal species that can only be found here are scattered. Though this country is home to over 52,000 species of animals, our country is considered as one 
of the countries that have a very large rate when it comes to discovering new species of animals in the world. With a little over 50% of the unique species here
in the country exist nowhere in the world but only here. 
  Below are list of some of the most prominent animals that can only be found in the country:

1.) Philippine Eagle 

  Scientific Name:  Pithecophaga jefferyi 
  Location:  Eastern Luzon, Samar, Leyte, and Mindanao 
  Status:  Endagered 
  Description:  
 The Philippine Eagle, scientifically known as Pithecophaga jefferyi, is an indigenous eagle species to the Philippines that is in grave risk of extinction.
 It is regarded as one of the biggest and most powerful eagles in the world and is also known as the "monkey-eating eagle." It has a strikingly long, shaggy crest, - 
 which contributes to its majestic appearance, and bright blue eyes.  This eagle, which can have a wingspan of up to 7 feet,is mostly found in the Philippines' rainforests, -
 particularly on the islands of Luzon, Samar, Leyte, and Mindanao. Despite serving as the Philippines' national emblem, the Philippine Eagle is threatened on a number of fronts, -
 including habitat loss, deforestation, hunting, and illegal trade, earning it a critical ranking on the IUCN Red List.

2.) Philippine Tarsier 

  Scientific Name:  Carlito syrichta / Tarsius syrichta 
  Location:  Bohol, Samar, Leyte, and Mindanao 
  Status:  Endagered 
  Description:  
 Tarsius syrichta, a small dwelling primate, also known as the Philippine tarsier, which is only found in the Philippines.
It is one of the tiniest primates in the world and is renowned for its large eyes, which each eye are around the same size of its brain. 
These nocturnal animals are known for their excellent leaping abilities and long fingers and toes, in which help them navigate through the dense forests. 

Bohol, Samar, Leyte, and Mindanao are the main Philippine islands where the Philippine tarsier can be found. These little primates are both inscetivorous - 
and carnivorous, relying mostly on insects such as grasshoppers and crickets for food. They are also very vocal and are capable to make wide range of vocalizations.
Sadly, it is also regarded as a vulnerable species and is protected by the Philippine law as a result of habitat degradation and illicit pet trade. To assist safeguard - 
these unsual and fascinating creatures and preserve their natural habitats, conservation efforts and initiatives have been implemented.

3.) Palawan Bearcat 

  Scientific Name:  Arctictis binturong whitei 
  Location:  Palawan 
  Status:  Possibly Endagered 
  Description:  
 The bearcat subspecies known as the Palawan bearcat, or Arctictis binturong whitei, is an animal only found in the Philippine -
island of Palawan. These enormous, arboreal beasts have a striking look that incorporates features of both cats and bears. A long, prehensile tail, shaggy black fur, - 
and a white face with distinguishing facial markings are the main characteristics of the Palawan bearcat. They spend a lot of time in trees and have powerful claws, - 
Making them great climbers. Frugivorous animals, Palawan bearcat eats primarily fruits but also small mammals, birds, and insects. 

Due to habitat loss, deforestation, hunting for food and pet trade, as well as habitat degradation, the Palawan bearcat is officially classified as vulnerable species
To safeguard their natural habitats and guarantee the survival of this distinctive and endagered subspecies, conservation initiatives are currently under progress.

4.) Mindoro Dwarf Buffalo-- 

  Scientific Name:  Bubalus mindorensis 
  Location:  Mindoro 
  Status:  Critically Endangered 
  Description:  
 The Bubalus mindorensis or tamaraw, also known as the Mindoro Dwarf Buffalo, is a diminutive and critically endagered breed of cattle -
that is only endigenous to the Philippine island of Mindoro. Although it shares a close relationship with the water buffalo, this species stands out due to its smaller size  
and its distinctive morphological traits. It is distinguished from other buffalo species due to its compact body, small legs, and curved horns that forms a V - shape. 
Tamaraws are mostly herbivorous animals that only eat grass and other types of flora. They are renowned for their elusive and reserved nature. 

The Mindoro Dwarf Buffalo is classified as highly endagered due to habitat degradation and hunting. Conservation efforts are being implimented to ensure this species'
existence and stop it from going extinct. These efforts include breeding programs and the creation of protected areas. The tamaraw has a cultural and ecological significance
for the region and is regarded as a national symbol of the Philippines.

5.) Philippine Forest Turtle 

  Scientific Name:  Siebenrockiella leytensis 
  Location:  Northern Palawan 
  Status:  Critically Endagered 
  Description:  
 The Siebenrockiella leytensis, or Philippine forest turtle, is an endemic species of freshwater turtle to the Philippines that is in grave
risk of extinction. These little turtles have an oval-shaped dark carapace(shell) with a stunning pattern of radiating yellow lines. they are commonly found in lowland woods 
and watery settings and are renowned for their elusive character. 
 
The habitat loss, illegal collection and pet trades are the main reasons why the Philippine forest turtly is one of the rarest and most endagered turtle species in the world.
To save this species form extinction, conservation efforts such as breeding programs and habitat protection are essential. Due to its severely endagered status, it is vital to
highlight that this turtle cannot be traded internationally, and efforts are being undertaken to stop the illegal pet trades in this species.

6.) Whale Sharks 

  Scientific Name:  Rhincondon typus 
  Location:  Sorsogon, Cebu, Bohol, etc. 
  Status:  Endagered 
  Description:  
 The whale shark, Rhincondon typus in scientific jargon, is the largest species of fish known to science and is distinguished by its gigantic size,
filter-feeding habits, and unusual looks. Warm, tropical waters, coral reefs and coastal areas are the frequent habitat for these gentle giants. 
Its large, flattened head and mouth, which can be up to several feet wide, are among its distinguishing characteristics, as it the checkerboard-like pattern of spots and
stripes on its back. Whale sharks are filter feeders, primarily ingesting planktons, small fish, and other microscopic creatures by swimming with their mouths open, which is -
unusual for its gigantic size. Due to their well-known calm demeanor, whale sharks are a popular subject for ecotourism and diving activities, where participants can swim alongside
these majestic giants.

Whale sharks are also classified as vulnerable species despite having a wide distribution, mostly because of habitat deterioration, unintentional bycatch in fishing activities, and
poaching. To safeguard and maintain these extraordinary and majestic species, conservation efforts are in place.

7.) Philippine Pangolin 

  Scientific Name:  Manis culionensis 
  Location:  Southwestern Philippines 
  Status:  Critically Endagered 
  Description:  
 The severly endagered Philippine pangolin, or Manis culionensis, is a type of pangolin that is native only to the Philippines. The distinctive, -
scale-covered mammals known as pangolins are distinguished by their armor like appearance. The only pangolin species to be found in the Philippines is the Philippine Pangolin.
These timid, nocturnal animals devour termites and ants, and other insects. Similar to human fingers, their bodies are covered in hard, overlapping scales compromised of keratin.
They can roll into a ball when threatened to shield their delicate underside. 

These harmless creatures are critically endagered due to habitat loss, illegal poaching for their highly priced scales which are used in traditional medicine, and as well as pet trades.
To ensure the continued existence of this species, conservation efforts are being made, including protecting its habitat and taking action againts illicit wildlife trade.

8.) Philippine Mouse-Deer 

  Scientific Name:  Tragulus nigricans 
  Location:  Balabac 
  Status:  Endagered 
  Description:  
 One of the tiniest ungulates in the world, the Philippine mouse-deer, Tragulus nigricans in Scientific jargon, is a specie indigenous to the Philippines.
These tiny deer are distinguished by their small stature, shy, private, and solitary habits. The reddish-brown coat of the Philippine mouse-deer is one of its distinguishing features,
along with its unusual white spots on its throat and bottom. They also feature long, flexible snouts, and tiny hooves, and skinny legs. They are good jumpers and agile enough to avoid
predators despite their small size. These creatures primarily consumes leaves, shoots, and fruits because they are herbivores. 

The Philippine mouse-deer is a vulnerable species because it is being hunted and its habitat is being lost. To preserve their habitats and guarantee the future of this unusual and
lovable species in the Philippines, conservation measures are currently under progress.

9.) Philippine Crocodile 

  Scientific Name:  Crocodylus mindorensis 
  Location:  Mindoro and Sierra Madre Natural Park 
  Status:  Critically Endagered 
  Description:  
 The Crocodylus mindorensis, or Philippine crocodile, is a critically endagered species of crocodile that is native to the Philippines. It is one of the mostly
critically endagered crocodile species in the world and stands out for being smaller that other crocodile species, with adults typically reaching
lengths of 2 to 3 meters. In the Philippines, river, marshes, and lakes are among the freshwater habitats where these crocodiles can be found.
They have a V-shaped ridge between their eyes and a large nose. The Philippine crocodile's coloring can range from grayish-green to brown. 

The survival of this species has been seriously threatened, mostly as a result of illicit fishing and hunting, and habitat loss. To save this severly -
endagered species and a unique biodiversity of the Philippines, conservation initiatives, such as captive breeding programs and habitat protections are essential and in place.

10.) Visayan Leopard Cat 

  Scientific Name:  Prionailurus javanensis sumatranus 
  Location:  Negros, Panay, and Cebu 
  Status:  Vulnerable 
  Description:  
 The Visayan leopard cat, or Prionailurus javanensis sumatranus in scientific jargon, is a diminutive wild cat subspecies found in the Visayan Island of the Philippines.
Its name refers to the leopard-like features of this elusive and nimble feline, including the characteristic spots and rosettes on its coat. 
Due to their nocturnal habits and reputation for seclusion and secrecy, Visayan leopard cats are mostly nocturnal animals. They are adept hunters
who target small mammals, birds, and reptiles for their prey. They thrive in dense woodlands and grasslands thanks to their small size, slender build,
and acute senses. 

The Visayan leopard cat is regarded as one of the most vulnerable animals because of habitat loss and hunting. Through Cconservation efforts, the natural
environment, preventing illegal poaching, and the continued existence of this rare and imperiled wild cat subspecies in the Philippines are all protected.
